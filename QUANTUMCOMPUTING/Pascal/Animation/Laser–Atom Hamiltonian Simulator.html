Physics notes

Ω (Rabi freq.) — proportional to the laser electric field amplitude × the dipole matrix element ⟨1|er|0⟩. Increasing laser power increases Ω linearly in field, quadratically in intensity. Sets the speed of rotation on the Bloch sphere.

δ (detuning) — when δ = 0 (resonance), the rotation axis is in the equatorial plane → full |0⟩↔|1⟩ inversion possible. Any δ ≠ 0 tilts the axis toward |0⟩, reducing max excitation to Ω²/(Ω²+δ²) and speeding up oscillations (Ω̃ > Ω). Also creates beat notes in the laser envelope.

φ (phase) — shifts the rotation axis azimuthally. φ=0 → rotation around X (X-gate). φ=π/2 → Y-gate. In hardware, this is set by an acousto-optic modulator (AOM) in nanoseconds. The phase is the cheapest parameter to control.

Gate recipe: choose Ω and δ, set φ to pick the axis, then pulse for time t = π/Ω̃ (π-pulse = full flip) or t = π/(2Ω̃) (Hadamard-like).
